## Executive Summary

Crisis communication is a 2026 commercial priority because the SEC's cybersecurity disclosure rule (Item 1.05 / Reg S-K 106) has converted communications quality into measurable regulatory exposure. The competitive SERP for the pillar term is dominated by generic templates from PR vendors (HubSpot, Smartsheet, Workshop, Asana) that lack regulatory depth and named-source authority. Risk Publishing's wedge is a research-led, regulator-anchored cluster that pairs every communications template with a specific compliance citation. The pillar is published; this strategy expands the pillar with nine supporting articles that share-claim long-tail traffic, build internal-link authority, and close gaps the SERP-1 group does not address.

**Cluster opportunity (estimated)**:
- Pillar keyword family captures ~5,000–8,000 US monthly searches.
- Supporting article keywords capture an additional ~12,000–18,000 monthly searches in aggregate.
- Featured-snippet capture realistic on at least 4 of the 10 pieces (numbered-list and definition formats).

### Competitive analysis (top 5)

- **HubSpot** (`blog.hubspot.com/service/crisis-communication-plan`): high authority, generic. Strong on examples and structure; weak on regulatory depth (no SEC 8-K coverage).
- **Smartsheet** (`smartsheet.com/content/crisis-communication-templates`): SaaS conversion hub. Templates only; no narrative depth or regulatory framing.
- **TechTarget** (`techtarget.com/searchdisasterrecovery/tip/5-tips-for-building-a-crisis-communication-plan`): IT-buyer focus; thin on PR/legal dimension.
- **Asana** (`asana.com/templates/crisis-management-plan`): conflates crisis management with crisis communications. SaaS template gallery with light editorial.
- **Workshop** (`useworkshop.com/resources/crisis-communications-plan-template-and-example/`): the most comparable comp; covers holding statements but lacks SEC and CIRCIA depth.

**Common gaps across SERP-1**:
- No SEC 8-K Item 1.05 / Item 8.01 distinction.
- No CIRCIA, sector-regulator, or state-breach-law overlay.
- No materiality framework for SEC reporting.
- No tie back to ISO 22301 §8.4 communication requirement.
- No "test your plan" / tabletop integration with BCM.

The pillar page already addresses every one of these gaps. The supporting cluster doubles down on each.
